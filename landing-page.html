What it's about

In the course we learn not to avoid, fight or quickly "resolve" conflicts, but to integrate them through conscious self-management, clear communication and a relationship-oriented attitude.

The training combines insights from psychology, neurobiology, mediation, somatic work, systemic counseling and elements of trauma work – and translates them into concrete, experiential practice.

The focus is not only on techniques, but on the ability to maintain tension, to clarify emotions, to take responsibility and to stay in touch – even when it gets difficult.

Who is this training for?

This basic training is aimed at:

All people who want to understand and change their conflict patterns

People in leadership positions (projects, organizations)

Multipliers (consulting, education, social fields)

People in relationships (couples, parents, co-parents, polyamorous relationships)

Participation requirements:

Curiosity, self-responsibility, and a willingness to engage in self-discovery and to confront the topic of conflict

No prior knowledge is required.

FAQs

What exactly is our approach to conflict competence with heart?

It's an experience-based approach to conflict resolution.

It understands conflicts not as problems that need to be solved, but as processes that require integration – in the body, in feelings, in thoughts, and in relationships.

In the training, you learn to consciously manage and shape conflicts, instead of avoiding, controlling, or escalating them.

Is the training more therapeutically or professionally oriented?

Both – and neither in the traditional sense.

The training is not therapy, but rather an educational and self-discovery experience.

The content is highly relevant to everyday life, relationships, organizations, and other professional contexts. Many participants experience this connection as a particular strength.

Do I need prior experience in coaching, mediation, or self-exploration?

No.

The training is designed to appeal to people without prior experience as well as experts.

What matters is not prior knowledge, but the willingness to engage in self-reflection and participate in experience-based learning processes.

How personal does it get in the groups?

As personal as feels right for you.

We work with presence, awareness, and dialogue – not with exposure or pressure.

You always decide how much you share. Safety, voluntariness, and self-responsibility are central principles of our work.

Does the training focus on specific situations from my life?

Yes, many exercises can only be directly applied to real-life situations in relationships, family, or work.

At the same time, you learn to recognize fundamental patterns that extend beyond individual conflicts. This allows the training to have a lasting effect on your life. You don't need to bring any other parties involved in the conflict.

What distinguishes this training from traditional communication or conflict seminars?

The focus is not on techniques or conversation models, but on integration.

We work in a body-based, emotionally conscious, and relationship-oriented way.

Communication arises from presence – not from correct behavior.

Can this training be recognized as continuing education?

Yes, depending on the professional context.

Isabel Kresse is a licensed mediator and trainer (BM) and is authorized to train others.

We will gladly issue a certificate of participation or training certificate upon request.

What happens if I can't attend an appointment?

The online sessions will be recorded and available to you.

However, we still recommend attending live, as the group processes are a key part of the learning experience.

Is the retreat mandatory?

No.

The retreat is an optional but recommended component for deepening and integration.

Many participants experience a significant qualitative step there.

Are there any further offers afterwards?

Yes.

The basic training is a prerequisite for more advanced courses, e.g., for managers, couples, or specific relationship contexts.

These advanced courses build upon the shared foundations and enable targeted further development.

Is the training also suitable for organizations or teams?

The basic training is aimed at individuals.

We offer separate formats for organizations, teams, or managers.

What if I realize that the training isn't right for me after all?

Then you can book the modules individually. Before the training begins, we conduct personal interviews. Should you find during this process that the training is not right for you, you will receive a refund of the costs minus a €100 processing fee.
